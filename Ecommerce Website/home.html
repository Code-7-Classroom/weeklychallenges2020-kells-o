Ragdoll Coat Colors

There are 4 coat types in Ragdoll cats: Traditional, Mink, Sepia, and Solid.

Mink vs. Traditional: Mink Ragdolls have a thicker, more fluffy coat than traditional Ragdolls, often with an extremely fluffy tail. A mink Ragdolls coat is smoother and much softer than traditional Ragdolls. Minks usually have aqua colored eyes instead of the blue eyes that the traditional Ragdolls have. Mink Ragdolls are born with their coat color and have aqua colored eyes by the time they are 12 weeks old. Traditional Ragdolls are born completely white and start slowly getting their color between 4 to 14 days old. Aqua eyes can vary from blue with just a hint of green to almost fully green eyes, but the most common is a very even blue green.

Sepia: Sepia Ragdolls have a much darker coat than mink Ragdolls. Sepia Ragdolls can have green, gold, blue, aqua blue, or a rainbow of other eye colors. Sepia Ragdolls have the thickest, fluffiest, and softest coats of all of the pointed Ragdolls.

Solid: Solid Ragdolls are the darkest and highest contrast of all the Ragdolls. Sepia, mink, and traditional Ragdolls are all pointed, and have different shades of color throughout their coat. A solid Ragdoll does not have this variation. Solid Ragdolls have a very soft, thick, fluffy coat, similar to mink and sepia Ragdolls. Solid Ragdolls can have green, gold, blue, aqua blue, or a rainbow of other eye colors.

Traditional, mink, and sepia Ragdolls' color continues to deepen and darken as they mature. Solid Ragdolls are born with full color, and they do not darken or lighten as they mature. Mink Ragdolls are RARE Sepia and solid Ragdolls are VERY RARE
